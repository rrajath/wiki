# thoughts.org — Personal Philosophy Essays

A long-running collection of personal reflections, philosophical explorations, and practical observations written 2020–2022. Captures how the author thinks about life, work, and identity.

## Major themes

### Mortality and perspective

Life is short in the context of cosmic scale (13.8 billion year universe; 80 year human life). Nothing "matters" in the absolute sense, which is liberating: spend the time on what genuinely gives joy rather than on approval or legacy. Referenced Marcus Aurelius and Seneca repeatedly.

### Stoicism in practice

Work stress, anxiety, and emotional reactions are the main sources of suffering — and all are voluntary. Sitting with thoughts, observing them without reacting, is the practice. Related: [[Stoicism]], [[Dichotomy of Control]], [[Accept Reality as It Is]].

### Procrastination

Procrastination is fundamentally a *starting problem*, not a finishing problem. Once a task is started, it gets done. The fix: force starting, not optimizing the whole system. Three questions to ask before any task: Should I do this? What happens if I don't? Can I delegate?

### Delegation

Delegation requires ongoing involvement, not abdication. If you delegate without checking in at each step, suboptimal decisions get made based on the delegate's priorities, not yours. Illustrated through a real estate purchase.

### Interest streams vs obsessions

- **Interest**: reading one book on a topic with reflection.
- **Obsession**: disproportionate multi-source consumption that crowds out other priorities.

Recommended approach: treat time and attention as currency. Choose a realistic number of interest streams. New interests go to a backlog — if still compelling after a month, swap in; otherwise, drop.

### Communication and updating models

Good communication requires three steps: explain your model → understand the other person's model → update by combining both. Skipping step 2 (understanding the other person's model) is the most common communication failure.

### Maps and models

Captured in resources.org in detail; cross-referenced here: models are necessary simplifications of reality, but mistaking the map for the territory is dangerous. Reality is the ultimate update; maps must be revised as the world changes.

### Writing and thinking

Writing improves thinking: it forces slow formulation, which forces better thinking. Thinking → speech → notes → published writing — each step adds delay and improves precision.

### Other concepts

- **Karma**: not a cosmic ledger but a social propagation mechanism — good behavior propagates outward and returns indirectly.
- **No absolutes, only trade-offs**: binary good/bad judgments are less useful than "did I do my best?"
- **Analysis paralysis**: spending more time thinking about the *right way* to do something than just doing it. The wrong structure is worse than no structure.
