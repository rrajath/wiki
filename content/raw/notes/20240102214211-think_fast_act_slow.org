#+title: Think Fast, Act Slow
#+filetags: :literature-note:


Most projects fail disastrously because people tend to Think Fast, Act Slow. This is because people have a [[id:47CB8B88-ED74-44F1-B36A-AE063098C7D8][Commitment Fallacy]] where they are very eager to commit to things. This could be because their boss asked them to do it, or they don’t want to be seen as the one asking questions and slowing things down. But for whatever reason, people tend to rush into things. That leads to poor planning, lot of unforseen circumstances that could have been avoided and hence, delays. This model never works and even if it does, the output quality would greatly suffer.
