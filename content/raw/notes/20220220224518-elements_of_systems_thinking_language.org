#+title: Elements of Systems Thinking Language
#+filetags: :literature-note:

- focuses on "closed interdependencies": with circular relationships
- offers a visual language: loop diagrams and other similar diagrams represent pictorially what the system looks like in a snapshot
- adds precision: reduces ambiguities and miscommunications with properly labeled parts by enforcing syntactical rules
- forces an "explicitness" of mental models
- allows examination and inquiry: by fostering a collective understanding of the problem, and defuses much of the defensiveness that arises from high-level debate
- emboies a worldview that looks at whole rather than parts: there's no us vs them kind of mentality, we're all part of the same system.

- Source: Systems Thinking Tools (p6)
