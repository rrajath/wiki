#+title: R and B loops
[2022-02-24 Thu 20:46]
Reinforcing loops compound change in one direction. Employee does well if his manager is supportive and does badly if manager is unsupportive.

Balancing loops try to maintain the state of a system by making adjustments. For example, too much or too little inventory is maintained by adjusting it with desired inventory.

These loops can coexist. In the employee-supervisor example, the employee's performance cannot infinitely grow. After a point it becomes a game of diminishing returns where the employee puts in longer hours and performance degrades. A balancing loop can come into picture and add breaks, vacations and other incentive to "maintain" the performance.
