#+title: Endometrium
#+filetags: :ivf:floating-note:

* This is the innermost layer of the uterus
* Consists of two layers:
** Basal layer
*** composed of stem cells
*** serves to regenerate the functional layer
*** remains relatively constant throughout the menstrual cycle
** Functional layer
*** adjacent to the uterine cavity
*** lined by a single layer of columnar epithelium
*** undergoes cyclic changes in response to hormones
* Functions of Endometrium:
** preparation of embryo implantation
*** thickens and becomes more vascular during the menstrual cycle
*** creates an optimal environment for the implantation of a fertilized egg
** support of pregnancy
*** if fertilization occurs, the endometrium transforms into a decidua
*** provides support and protection for the developing fetus
** menstruation
*** in the absence of pregnancy, the functional layer is shed
** maintenance of uterine integrity
*** prevents adhesions between opposed walls of the myometrium
*** maintains the patency of the uterine cavity
** regulation of menstrual cycle
*** responds to hormonal cycle, particularly estrogen and progesterone
*** undergoes cyclic regeneration from stem cells in the basal layer
* Endometrium undergoes cyclic changes throughout the menstrual cycle - with [[id:6BB6C3BB-1963-4DDE-9782-32A817F6B239][Proliferative Phase]] and [[id:5B745668-F3C6-43A5-9271-E05E68D4A305][Secretory Phase]]
* If pregnancy doesn't occur, the endometrium is shed during menstruation and the cycle starts over.
