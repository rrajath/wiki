#+title: You always make the best decision
#+filetags: :permanent-note:

No matter what, you always make the best decision. It may not be the right decision, it may not even be close to the right decision. But at that moment, you think it’s the best decision. Your brain quickly weighs pros and cons before making that decision and picks what it thinks is the best move forward. Later when you look back, you may feel like it wasn’t a good bet. But at that moment, you think (or your brain thinks) you made the right decision. This can happen at any level - financial planning where you need to project years into the future. It can even happen at the most granular level - what you decide to do the very next second.
