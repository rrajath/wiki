#+title: Regulating the menstrual cycle
#+filetags: :ivf:floating-note:

* This refers to the process of establishing or maintaining a predictable and consistent pattern of menstruation. This involves:
** Hormonal balance - [[id:C1F531B2-3BB4-4BA2-93D6-96CE3EA06971][Estrogen]] and [[id:F9B0D899-1538-468A-9977-D298815DE6EA][Progesterone]] work together to:
*** prepare the uterine lining for potential pregnancy
*** control the timing of the ovulation
*** trigger menstruation if pregnancy does not occur
* There are two phases in a menstrual cycle - [[id:6BB6C3BB-1963-4DDE-9782-32A817F6B239][Proliferative Phase]] and [[id:5B745668-F3C6-43A5-9271-E05E68D4A305][Secretory Phase]]. They refer to specific stages in the endometrial development in the uterus.
* [[id:C1F531B2-3BB4-4BA2-93D6-96CE3EA06971][Estrogen]] dominates the first half and [[id:F9B0D899-1538-468A-9977-D298815DE6EA][Progesterone]] dominates the second half
