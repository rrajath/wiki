# Derek Sivers

Musician, entrepreneur, and author. Founder of CD Baby (1998–2008), one of the first online music stores for independent musicians. Sold CD Baby for $22 million in 2008 and donated the proceeds to music education charities. Subsequently traveled internationally, wrote prolifically, and maintained a deliberately minimal lifestyle.

## Key contributions

**CD Baby** — built from scratch to serve independent musicians who had no mainstream distribution channel. Famous for its unusually personal, humorous customer emails (especially the shipping confirmation), which became an early viral marketing case study.

**Books** — *Anything You Want* (business philosophy), *Hell Yeah or No* (decision-making), *How to Live* (philosophical exploration of multiple life philosophies). All are short and deliberately structured.

**TED talk: "How to Start a Movement"** — a 3-minute talk using footage of a lone dancer at a festival. Sivers argues that the *first follower* is more important than the originator: the first person to join transforms a "lone nut" into a leader, and makes it safe for others to follow. See [[First Follower]].

## Ideas widely cited

- **[[Hell Yeah or No]]** — binary filter for commitments: if not a resounding "hell yeah", say no
- **[[Ideas as Multipliers]]** — ideas are multipliers; execution is the actual value
- **[[First Follower]]** — first follower principle in movement dynamics
- "The standard pace is for chumps" — conventional timelines are not binding constraints
- "Obvious to you. Amazing to others" — expertise blindness; what feels mundane to an expert is genuinely valuable to others
- "Detailed dreams blind you to new means" — over-specification of goals prevents noticing better paths
