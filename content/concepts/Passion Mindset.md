# Passion Mindset

A career orientation that focuses on **what value the world is offering you** — waiting for an innate passion to surface from the opportunities you encounter. From Cal Newport's *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]*.

## Core stance

A person with the passion mindset believes there is a pre-existing calling for them, and the task is to discover it. They evaluate each role or opportunity by whether it "sparks" that passion.

## Why it leads to dissatisfaction

- Passion rarely arrives fully formed before skill. It typically emerges *after* mastery.
- Constantly asking "Is this my passion?" draws attention to every gap between the job and an idealized vision, amplifying dissatisfaction.
- The mindset is passive — waiting for the world to deliver, rather than building something rare to trade.

## Contrast

The [[Craftsman Mindset]] is the productive alternative: focus on what you can build and offer, not on what you hope to receive. See *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]* for the full argument.
