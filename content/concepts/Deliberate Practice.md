# Deliberate Practice

An approach to skill development where you deliberately stretch your abilities **beyond your comfort zone** and then receive **ruthless feedback** on your performance. Originated in research by Anders Ericsson; applied to careers by Cal Newport in *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]*.

## What makes it deliberate

Ordinary practice (repeating what you already do well) produces a performance plateau. Deliberate practice breaks that plateau by:

1. Targeting a specific weakness or frontier, not overall performance.
2. Working at the edge of current ability — uncomfortable, not automatic.
3. Getting immediate, granular feedback — not vague praise or silence.
4. Repeating with adjustments until the weakness is eliminated.

## Role in career development

Deliberate practice is the mechanism through which the [[Craftsman Mindset]] converts time and effort into [[Career Capital]]. Without it, hard work accumulates experience but not necessarily skill.

## Contrast with naive practice

| Naive practice | Deliberate practice |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Repeat familiar tasks | Target specific weaknesses |
| Comfortable | Uncomfortable |
| Vague feedback | Granular, immediate feedback |
| Performance plateau | Continuous improvement |
