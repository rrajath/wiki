# Make Little Bets

A strategy of running small, low-cost experiments to discover promising directions before committing fully. Referenced in *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]* as part of building [[Career Capital]].

## Core idea

Rather than betting your career on a single hypothesis about what will be valuable or fulfilling, you run many small bets in parallel or sequence. Each bet is cheap to run and cheap to abandon if it fails.

A little bet:
- Has a clear, short time horizon.
- Tests a specific question (Will I enjoy this? Is there demand for this?)
- Produces concrete signal — not just a feeling, but evidence.

## Role in capital building

Little bets add incremental [[Career Capital]] even when they don't pan out, because you learn what not to pursue. Successful bets reveal leverage points worth investing in more heavily.

## Analogy

Like a venture portfolio: most experiments produce modest signal, a few produce outsized direction. The key is keeping individual bets small enough that failure is survivable and fast.
