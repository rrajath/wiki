# Skill Stacking

Combining two or more skills you are reasonably good at to create a unique competitive position that neither skill alone would provide.

## Core idea

You do not need to be the world's best at anything. Being top-25% in two or more complementary domains creates a combination that is rare and hard to replicate. The intersection is where leverage lives.

> "Astronomers figure he must be an exceptional programmer, since he's clearly a mediocre astronomer. While programmers figure he must be an exceptional astronomer, since his programming is strictly middle-of-the-road!"

The cross-domain person is underestimated by each domain's insiders — and uniquely valuable because of the combination.

## Examples

- Programmer + runner → performance optimization for athletic apps, health tech.
- Engineer + writer → technical communication, developer advocacy.
- Developer + domain expert (medicine, law, finance) → tools that people in that domain actually want.

## How it differs from [[Career Capital]]

Career Capital focuses on building deep, rare skill in one area (usually within one career capital market). Skill stacking is particularly suited to **auction markets** (see [[Career Capital Market]]) where combinations are valued over dominance in a single dimension.

## Identifying your stack

- What are you already in the top quartile at?
- What would you enjoy bringing to top-quartile?
- What combinations exist at the intersection of those that have demand but low supply?

The hard part is finding the combination. The rewarding part is that once found, it is very difficult for others to replicate quickly.
