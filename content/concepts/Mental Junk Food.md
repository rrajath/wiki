# Mental Junk Food

Information consumed passively and indiscriminately — stimulating in the moment but nutritionally empty, leaving the same "shitty" feeling that junk food does after eating.

## What qualifies

- Information not deliberately sought out for a specific purpose.
- Streams of content (social media feeds, YouTube autoplay, news headlines) rather than intentional deep reads.
- Content that is neither informative nor actionable for the person consuming it.

The key distinction: you did not go looking for it; it arrived and you consumed it because it was there and stimulating.

## Why it feels bad

The brain gets the dopamine hit of new stimulation but none of the reward of genuine learning or progress. The result mirrors physical junk food: a spike followed by a crash — tired, overstimulated, unaccomplished.

## Relationship to [[Information Overload]]

Mental junk food is a primary vector for information overload: passive streams deliver high volume with low signal density. The brain processes all of it (filtering has a cost) while retaining almost none of it usefully.

## The alternative

[[Instant Gratification vs Deep Satisfaction]] — deliberately choosing content and engagement that leads to real learning or creation, even when it requires more initial effort.
