# Our Defaults

The behaviors the brain executes automatically, without deliberate choice — what you do when you are not actively directing attention. We run on autopilot most of the time, and the autopilot runs defaults.

## What defaults are

Defaults are deeply grooved behavioral patterns — the path of least resistance in any situation. They form through repetition: whatever you do frequently enough becomes automatic. The brain optimizes for efficiency by caching repeated sequences as defaults, freeing deliberate attention for other tasks.

This is structurally similar to [[Muscle Memory]] but applies to broader behavioral patterns, not just motor sequences.

## Why defaults matter

The vast majority of daily behavior is driven by defaults, not deliberate choice. If your defaults are well-calibrated — exercise, focused work, intentional reading — they compound positively. If they are poorly calibrated — doom-scrolling, reactive email, passive consumption — they compound negatively.

[[Instant Gratification vs Deep Satisfaction]]: the brain's natural default gravitates toward instant gratification because it is the path of lowest immediate resistance. The default must be deliberately overridden to choose deep work.

## Changing defaults

Defaults are sticky but not fixed. Changing them requires:
1. **[[Awareness]]** — noticing the default pattern in action, in real time.
2. **Repeated deliberate intervention** — consistently choosing the new behavior until it becomes the groove.
3. **Environmental design** — making the desired behavior easier (lower friction) and the default behavior harder (higher friction).

The goal of most productivity and habit systems is to make good behavior the new default — to make the autopilot work for you rather than against you.
