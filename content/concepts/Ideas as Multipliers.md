# Ideas as Multipliers

A mental model by [[entities/Derek Sivers]] that reframes the value of ideas: an idea alone is worth nothing; it is a *multiplier* applied to execution.

## The formula

Sivers' original formulation:

| Idea quality | × | Execution | = | Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Awful | × | No execution | = | $0 |
| Good | × | Weak | = | $10K |
| Good | × | So-so | = | $100K |
| Good | × | Good | = | $1M |
| Brilliant | × | No execution | = | $0 |
| Brilliant | × | Good | = | $10M |
| Brilliant | × | Great | = | $100M+ |

The key insight: multiplying any value by zero gives zero. A brilliant idea with no execution has the same value as a terrible idea with no execution.

## What this means in practice

1. **Don't protect ideas** — sharing an idea costs nothing because ideas alone create no value. Only execution creates value, and execution is hard to copy.
2. **Start before you're ready** — the sooner execution begins, the sooner the multiplier activates.
3. **Execution is the scarce resource** — time, attention, follow-through. Ideas are abundant; execution is not.
4. **The differentiator is the person, not the concept** — two people with the same idea will produce very different outcomes based on how they execute.

## Relationship to other concepts

- [[Craftsman Mindset]] / [[Deliberate Practice]] — the mechanism for improving execution quality over time
- [[Make Little Bets]] — small execution experiments to test whether an idea's execution is tractable before committing fully
- [[Career Capital]] — execution quality compounds into career capital; ideas do not
