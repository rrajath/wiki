# IVF Pregnancy Complications

IVF pregnancies are classified as high-risk. Several complications have elevated incidence compared to natural conception.

## General placenta issues

- Age is a significant risk factor, especially over 35.
- OBs typically recommend not going past 39 weeks for IVF pregnancies.
- Open questions: what specific placental issues arise? Why is >39 weeks risky?

## Placenta Previa

The placenta attaches low in the uterus, partially or fully covering the cervix.

- More common in IVF pregnancies.
- Requires additional ultrasound monitoring.
- High chance of resolving spontaneously as the uterus grows.
- Open questions: when does it typically show up? What scans are used? What are the symptoms and resolution pathways?

## Preeclampsia

A serious pregnancy complication characterized by high blood pressure and organ dysfunction.

- Occurs around 20 weeks into pregnancy.
- Life-threatening if untreated; affects 3–7% of pregnancies.
- IVF pregnancies carry elevated risk.

## Vasa Previa

Rare (1 in ~200 IVF pregnancies) but serious: fetal blood vessels run through the membranes near the cervix, unprotected by the placenta or umbilical cord.

- Risk of catastrophic fetal bleeding if membranes rupture.
- Only reliable treatment: inpatient hospitalization as delivery approaches.
- Open questions: mechanism of occurrence, symptoms to watch for, full risk picture.

## Heart Echo (Echocardiogram)

IVF carries a small increased risk of congenital heart defects. As part of IVF pregnancy monitoring, a fetal or maternal echocardiogram may be ordered.
