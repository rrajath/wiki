# Commitment Fallacy

The tendency to rush into commitment — to a project, plan, or course of action — even when deeper reflection would reveal it as premature or unwise. From *How Big Things Get Done*.

## Why it happens

- Pressure from superiors: "Just say yes and figure it out later."
- Fear of being seen as the blocker or the one asking too many questions.
- Social dynamics that reward apparent confidence over actual preparedness.

## The consequence

Premature commitment leads to [[Think Slow Act Fast|Think Fast, Act Slow]]: execution begins before understanding is complete. The result is delays, rework, and poor quality output — precisely the bad outcome the rapid commitment was meant to avoid.

## The remedy

Resist the social pressure to commit before you have the information to commit wisely. The short-term social cost of asking more questions is almost always lower than the long-term cost of building the wrong thing fast.

See [[Think Slow Act Fast]] for the full framework.
