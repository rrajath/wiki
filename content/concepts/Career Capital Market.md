# Career Capital Market

The competitive landscape in which you deploy [[Career Capital]]. Coined by Cal Newport in *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]*.

## Two market types

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Winner-take-all** | One dominant skill; everyone competes for the same capital | TV screenwriting — only script quality matters |
| **Auction** | Multiple capital types accepted; individuals can build unique combinations | Software engineering — depth + domain + communication can combine uniquely |

## Why it matters for strategy

- In a **winner-take-all** market, identify the one capital and invest in it exclusively. Spreading effort across many skills is fatal.
- In an **auction** market, look for rare combinations that have high value but low supply. Skill stacking across adjacent domains is a viable moat.

Misreading your market leads to wasted investment: trying to build a unique portfolio when everyone is evaluated on a single axis, or narrowing down when breadth is actually the advantage.
