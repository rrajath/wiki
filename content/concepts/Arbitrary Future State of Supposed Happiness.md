# Arbitrary Future State of Supposed Happiness

The vague future condition we tell ourselves will finally make us happy — a moving target that recedes as we approach it.

## What it actually is

"Happiness" used this way is not just a pleasant emotion. It is a composite of fulfillment, calm, and an accepting state of mind — peace with how things are. The problem is that we rarely define it clearly, making it abstract and subjective.

Most often, we assume happiness will come from changing the current state to a more favorable one. But two things reliably undermine this:

1. **The state is not clearly defined** — we don't know what will actually satisfy us.
2. **Arrival doesn't satisfy** — once the desired state is reached, we immediately identify the next "if only" condition and the goalpost moves.

## Why we keep chasing it

The chase is driven by the same mechanism as the [[Dichotomy of Control]] confusion: believing that external circumstances (the right job, the right relationship, the right outcome) produce internal states. But internal peace is not produced by external configuration.

## The alternative

[[Accept Reality as It Is]] — not as a resignation, but as recognition that peace is available now, in the present, without requiring anything to change first. Happiness in the deeper sense is a mode of engagement with the present, not a reward for achieving a particular future state.
