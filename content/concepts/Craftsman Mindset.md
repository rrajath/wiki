# Craftsman Mindset

A career orientation that focuses relentlessly on **what you can offer the world** — the quality and rarity of your output — rather than on what the world owes you. From Cal Newport's *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]*.

## Core stance

A craftsman shows up and asks: *"How can I get better at this?"* The work itself, and the standard of excellence, are the north star.

This contrasts with the [[Passion Mindset]], which asks: *"Is this work fulfilling me?"* — a question that tends to spiral into dissatisfaction because no job perfectly delivers on that promise.

## What it requires

[[Deliberate Practice]] is the craftsman's primary method. Not comfortable repetition — deliberately working at the edge of ability, tracking performance, and seeking feedback that stings.

Building [[Career Capital]] is the outcome. The craftsman mindset is the orientation that drives capital accumulation.

## Why it works

The craftsman mindset generates a virtuous cycle:

```mermaid
flowchart LR
    CM[Craftsman Mindset] --> DP[Deliberate Practice]
    DP --> Skill[Rare Skill]
    Skill --> CC[Career Capital]
    CC --> Autonomy[Autonomy & Mission]
    Autonomy --> Passion[Passion / Engagement]
```

Passion is the *output* of mastery, not its prerequisite.
