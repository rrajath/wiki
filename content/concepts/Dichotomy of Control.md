# Dichotomy of Control

A core [[Stoicism|Stoic]] principle: everything that happens can be divided into what is within your control and what is not.

## The two categories

| In your control | Outside your control |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Your judgments | Other people's actions |
| Your intentions | External events |
| Your reactions | Outcomes |
| Your effort | Reputation |

A vast majority of things fall in the "outside your control" column. Suffering and frustration largely come from treating the uncontrollable as if it were controllable.

## The practice

1. When facing a situation, explicitly ask: *Is this in my control?*
2. If yes — act.
3. If no — accept it and redirect energy to what you can influence.

## Connection to happiness

Striving for control over external outcomes is a path to [[Arbitrary Future State of Supposed Happiness]] — perpetually chasing a state that recedes as you approach it. Focusing on your own responses is the stoic path to equanimity.
