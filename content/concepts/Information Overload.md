# Information Overload

The state of being exposed to more information than the brain can effectively process, leading to degraded decision quality, [[Decision Fatigue]], and a sense of overwhelm.

## Mechanism

When the brain is bombarded with a high volume of information, it struggles to distinguish signal from noise — to identify which pieces are actually salient. The result is cognitive exhaustion without proportional understanding or progress.

Information overload is a primary driver of [[Mental Junk Food]] consumption: when overwhelmed, the path of least resistance is more passive, low-quality input rather than rest or focused engagement.

## Handling information overload

A three-category system for structuring information intake:

1. **Books** — a fixed number per month. Bounded, deliberate, deep.
2. **One obsession at a time** — instead of juggling many interests simultaneously, pick one topic and go deep. Prevents the scattered feeling of wanting to learn everything at once.
3. **Scheduled frivolous time** — newsletters, YouTube, social feeds get a specific time slot rather than bleeding into the whole day. Bounded shallow input.

The goal is not zero information but intentional information: you choose what enters and when, rather than letting the stream flow unchecked.

## Connection to defaults

Without active management, [[Our Defaults]] take over: the brain reaches for whatever is most available, which is usually the most stimulating and least nutritious. [[Awareness]] of current cognitive state is the first step toward intervention.
