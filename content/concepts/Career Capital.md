# Career Capital

Rare and valuable skills that give you leverage to demand a fulfilling career. The term comes from Cal Newport's *[[So Good They Can't Ignore You]]*.

## Core idea

Career Capital is the "currency" you trade for great work conditions — autonomy, creativity, impact, a mission you care about. You can't demand those things without something valuable to offer in return.

Examples:
- A television writer's capital is the ability to write compelling scripts.
- A software engineer's capital might be systems design expertise, a specific domain (ML, distributed systems), or a rare combination of skills.

## How it's built

Capital is accumulated through [[Deliberate Practice]] — deliberately working at the edge of your abilities and getting ruthless feedback. Merely showing up and working hard produces a performance plateau.

You also grow capital by [[Make Little Bets|making little bets]]: small experiments that probe new directions and reveal which investments compound fastest.

## Where to invest it

The right investment depends on your [[Career Capital Market]] — whether you are in a winner-take-all market (one dominant skill matters) or an auction market (unique combinations win).

## Relationship to passion

Passion does not precede capital — it follows it. Mastery creates engagement, not the other way around. See [[Craftsman Mindset]] vs [[Passion Mindset]].
