# Accept Reality as It Is

Reality exists independent of your opinion of it. It is a cascade of cause and effect. It does not respond to your preferences, and calling it "unfair" is not a description of reality but of your reaction to it.

## Core idea

The universe does not care about your feelings about it. Events happen. What you label as unfair, random, or unjust is simply what happened — no more, no less. Attaching the labels "unfair" or "unjust" is an emotional response that adds a layer of suffering on top of the situation.

Accepting reality as it is does not mean passivity. It means:
1. Seeing clearly what is actually happening without distortion.
2. Acting from that clear seeing rather than from resistance and denial.

## Corollary: [[Dichotomy of Control]]

Once you accept what is actually happening, the next step is identifying what you can and cannot change. Acceptance enables that clarity; resistance to reality obscures it.

## Why it's difficult

We naturally resist reality when it contradicts our preferences or self-image. We are caught up in self-involvement ("this shouldn't be happening to me") rather than seeing the situation as it is.

## Connection to [[Attaching Identity to Outcomes]]

When we attach identity to outcomes, we resist reality because accepting "the project failed" feels like accepting "I failed." Separating identity from outcome makes reality easier to accept and act on.
